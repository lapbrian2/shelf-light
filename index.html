About This World

A flat illustration given depth and life.

This started as a single illustration — a quiet cafe-bookshop, seen from the inside. Warm sage walls, rows of books, two cups of coffee on a long table. A still image with a feeling you couldn't quite walk into.

Now you can. Every surface, every color, every gestural pen mark has been rebuilt as a 3D space. The hand-drawn sketch lines float above the scene like an artist's trace — slightly off-register, exactly the way they'd land if you drew over a reference by hand.

The teal bar counter anchors the entire composition. It's the only high-saturation element in the room — one bold chromatic choice that makes everything else feel muted and warm by contrast. That's the kind of decision that makes a world feel intentional.

SpaceSingle-point perspective. You're seated at the table, looking into the room. The vanishing point pulls your eye toward the door.
FormBoxes everywhere — shelves, counter, chair frames, book spines. Hard geometry. No curves, no softness. The illustration's shape language, preserved.
TexturePerfectly clean fills underneath. The sketch lines above are the only texture — gestural marks that draw in as you arrive, like watching the artist work.
LightThere are no lights. The warmth you feel comes from the palette itself and the way the colors interact. Every shadow is a shape, placed by hand.
ColorEight colors. Each one assigned to a region. No blending, no gradients. One teal anchor at full saturation against a muted world.
MotionThe pendant lights sway. The camera drifts imperceptibly. Scroll to move deeper into the room. The scene is alive but unhurried.

The Journey

01 — The Source

It Started as a Single Illustration

One image · One feeling · One afternoon

A cafe-bookshop drawn in flat colors and gestural pen lines. The kind of illustration that makes you want to sit down, order something, and stay a while. The question was: what if you actually could?

02 — The Space

Building the Room

Walls · Shelves · Table · Chairs

Every flat surface in the original becomes a 3D plane. Bookshelves line the back wall. A long table with coffee cups sits in the center. Four chairs, slightly turned, like someone just stood up. The illustration's geometry, given real depth.

03 — The Lines

Drawing Over the 3D

Hand-drawn marks · Floating above the scene

The sketch lines aren't part of the 3D. They hover above it — a separate layer of gestural pen marks that draw themselves in when you arrive. Slightly imprecise, slightly drifted from the geometry underneath. That imperfection is what makes it feel human.

04 — The Color

Eight Colors. No Gradients.

One palette · One world · No blending

Eight hex values define the entire world. Sage for the walls, salmon for warmth, cream for the brightest surfaces. And one bold teal bar counter that anchors the whole composition — the single high-saturation choice that makes everything else feel quiet.

05 — The Light

No Light Sources. Just Color.

The warmth comes from the palette itself

There are no lights in this scene. No lamps, no sun, no ambient glow. The warmth you feel is entirely the palette — warm sage against cool teal, salmon against cream. The shadows are shapes placed by hand, not computed. Every shadow is a deliberate artistic choice.

06 — The Life

Scroll to Move Through It

Camera choreography · Pendant sway · Warmth shifts

The pendants sway gently. The camera drifts like breathing. As you scroll, you move deeper into the room — the perspective shifts, the warmth changes. It's unhurried, the way a real cafe afternoon feels. The illustration is alive now.

How It's Built

No frameworks.
No abstractions.
Pure web.

This entire world — the 3D scene, the animations, the scroll choreography, the hand-drawn overlay — is built with raw browser APIs pushed to their edges. No React, no Vue. Vite bundles the modules, but every line of logic is vanilla JavaScript talking directly to WebGL, the Web Audio API, and the DOM.

Three.js renders the scene. GSAP drives the scroll-triggered camera moves. Lenis makes the scrolling feel physical. The sketch lines are SVG paths that draw themselves in when you arrive. Every color in the 3D scene matches the original illustration's palette exactly. The pendant lights sway on a sine wave. The exposure shifts warmer as you scroll deeper. No lights — the warmth comes from the colors themselves.
