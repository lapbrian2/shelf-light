## Pattern: Scroll-Triggered Storytelling

Camera-driven narrative. The user scrolls and the 3D cafe world unfolds as a continuous pullback from seated-at-table to doorway. Each scroll section is a "chapter" with progressive disclosure.

**Conversion strategy**: Narrative scroll increases time-on-page 3x. Scroll progress indicator already implemented. Commission form is the climax CTA at the end.

**Section structure**:
1. Hero hook (close-up, seated in cafe)
2. About (the illustration, cups steaming)
3. Palette (color system, teal bar)
4. Journey (building the room, open book)
5. How (pure web, bookshelf + plant)
6. CTA climax (wide shot, commission form)

---

## Style: Motion-Driven + Parallax Storytelling

**Primary style**: Motion-Driven (animation-heavy, microinteractions, scroll effects, parallax, page transitions)
**Secondary**: Parallax Storytelling (layered scrolling, cinematic, scroll-triggered, progressive disclosure)
**Tertiary**: 3D & Hyperrealism (depth, spatial navigation, tactile, immersive)

## Typography

### Current

- DM Sans (Google Fonts) — body, headings
- Italic variant for accent text ("Get a world.")

### UI Pro Max Recommendation

**Archivo / Space Grotesk** pairing recommended for creative portfolios.

However, the current DM Sans choice works well for the warm, approachable cafe aesthetic. A dual-font pairing would add typographic hierarchy:
- **Headings**: DM Sans 700 (or switch to Archivo Black for more punch)
- **Body**: DM Sans 400
- **Accent/Labels**: Consider a monospace or hand-drawn font for section labels ("ABOUT THIS WORLD", "THE JOURNEY")

## What's Missing for Awwwards

Based on the style analysis (Motion-Driven + Parallax Storytelling + 3D Hyperrealism), these are the gaps between current state and Awwwards submission quality, ranked by impact:

### Tier 1: High Impact (Would Score Points in Every Category)

#### 1. Character-Level Text Animation
**Current**: Word-level split reveal (fade + blur)
**Awwwards standard**: Character-level staggered animation with individual letter transforms (translateY, rotation, blur). Each letter slides up from below with a slight rotation and blur that resolves. GSAP SplitText or manual char splitting.

#### 2. Page Load Transition / Reveal
**Current**: Loader bar fills, elements fade in with timed delays
**Awwwards standard**: The 3D scene itself IS the reveal — camera starts zoomed into a book spine or coffee cup (extreme close-up, fills viewport), then pulls back to reveal the cafe. This makes the "backing out the door" concept work from the very first moment. The loader becomes part of the experience, not separate from it.

#### 3. Post-Processing Shaders
**Current**: Raw Three.js render with ACES Filmic tone mapping
**Awwwards standard**: EffectComposer with subtle film grain, chromatic aberration on mouse movement, and vignette. These are the "I can't explain why this feels premium" touches. Even very subtle values (grain intensity 0.04, aberration 0.002) make a massive difference.

#### 4. Scroll Velocity Effects
**Current**: Static scrub speed (1.8)
**Awwwards standard**: Lenis provides scroll velocity. Use it to: skew content slightly during fast scroll (CSS skewY based on velocity), add motion blur to the 3D scene during fast scroll, or slow the camera movement during very slow scroll for a "studying the details" feel.

### Tier 2: Medium Impact (Differentiators)

#### 5. Cursor Interaction with 3D Scene
**Current**: Mouse parallax offsets camera position
**Awwwards standard**: Raycasting on hover — books tilt toward cursor, cups wobble, pendant lights follow more dramatically. The feeling that "my cursor affects this world" is a major Awwwards differentiator.

#### 6. Sound Design Tied to Scroll Position
**Current**: Static pink noise toggle (on/off)
**Awwwards standard**: Audio evolves with scroll position — cups clinking near About, page turning near Journey, door bell at CTA. Crossfade between ambient layers using scroll position as mix parameter.

#### 7. Magnetic Buttons / Elastic Hover
**Current**: Standard hover states
**Awwwards standard**: CTA buttons that magnetically attract toward the cursor when nearby, with elastic spring-back. GSAP spring physics or custom implementation.

#### 8. Section Divider Animations
**Current**: Static horizontal lines
**Awwwards standard**: Dividers that draw themselves as they enter viewport (SVG strokeDashoffset), or morph from one shape to another, or split apart to reveal the next section.

### Tier 3: Polish (Attention to Detail)

#### 9. prefers-reduced-motion Support
**Critical for accessibility scoring**. Wrap all animations in a media query check. Provide a static, non-animated experience as fallback. Awwwards judges specifically check for this.

#### 10. Custom Scrollbar
Match the scrollbar to the cafe palette. Or hide it entirely and rely on the scroll progress bar.

#### 11. View Transitions API
For internal navigation (nav links jumping to sections), use the View Transitions API for a smooth cross-fade instead of instant scroll. Progressive enhancement — falls back gracefully.

#### 12. OG Image / Social Preview
A rendered screenshot of the cafe scene as the Open Graph image. Makes link previews on LinkedIn/Twitter show the 3D world instead of a blank card.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Corporate templates (generic layouts, stock photo grids)
- Generic cookie-cutter animations (everything fading in identically)
- Performance without purpose (heavy effects that don't serve the narrative)
- Ignoring mobile (Awwwards judges on mobile too — simplified scene is fine, broken is not)
- Over-animating (every element moving competes for attention — pick focal moments)
